Discover Montevideo

Demographics

Montevideo is Uruguay's largest city, home to over 1.3 million people, making it the cultural and economic heartbeat of the nation. The city's diverse population reflects Uruguay's history as a melting pot of European, African, and indigenous influences, creating a vibrant and inclusive atmosphere.

Statistical Data

  • GDP Contribution: Montevideo contributes significantly to Uruguay's GDP, accounting for approximately 50% of the nation's economic output.
  • Economic Sectors: The city's economy is driven by various sectors, including finance, technology, tourism, and services. The financial district in Ciudad Vieja houses numerous banks, investment firms, and corporate offices.
  • Employment Opportunities: Montevideo offers a range of employment opportunities across industries, with a focus on information technology, finance, hospitality, and creative industries.
  • Foreign Investment: The city attracts foreign investment due to its strategic location, stable economy, skilled workforce, and supportive business environment.

Events and Festivals

Montevideo is renowned for its lively events and festivals that celebrate its rich cultural heritage. From the vibrant Carnival, a colorful extravaganza of music, dance, and costumes, to the Montevideo Tango Festival, showcasing the passion and elegance of Argentine tango, there's always something exciting happening in the city.
